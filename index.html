How this estimate is built

Method in plain language

This tool follows the analog / nearest-neighbor approach recommended for sparse small-event regions: rather than a black-box score, it finds the historical Region 5 disasters whose hazard signature most resembles your event and anchors the estimate to what was actually obligated in those events.

  • Targets (real): Public Assistance obligated dollars and Individual Assistance (IHP) approved dollars are pulled live from OpenFEMA — the FemaWebDisasterSummaries and DisasterDeclarationsSummaries v2 datasets, filtered to Region 5 major (DR) declarations.
  • Similarity: your wind / hail / flood-crest / rainfall inputs are normalized and compared to each past event with a Gaussian kernel, with a bonus for matching incident type. The closest events become the analog set.
  • Magnitude: each analog's obligations are converted to a per-capita rate (using its impacted-county footprint), similarity-weighted, and re-scaled to your affected population. The interval spans the spread of the nearest analogs — wide intervals are honest for this long-tailed, skewed problem.
  • Thresholds: the estimate is compared to FEMA's published per-capita and large-project indicators to flag what a request might trigger and the dollar gap to each line.
Important limitations (read this)
  • Hazard metrics are modeled proxies. OpenFEMA records the dollars but not the wind/hail/flood/rainfall of each event. Those signatures are derived here from the declaration's incident type, narrative title, and obligation scale. A production model joins NOAA Storm Events, USGS streamgages, and hazard swaths for true measured intensity.
  • Obligations lag and reconcile over years. PA totals for recent disasters are systematically under-counted until projects mature; the data is raw FAC-Trax/Grants Manager output subject to human error and is not FEMA's official financial system.
  • Skewed, modest accuracy. Disaster cost is zero-inflated and long-tailed; peer-reviewed damage models explain under half the variance. Treat point estimates as order-of-magnitude with wide intervals — especially for rare severe events the model extrapolates beyond.
  • Selection & regime. Only declared events appear, and declaration is partly bureaucratic. Pending FEMA reform (parametric "RAPID" PA, higher thresholds) could change how dollars flow.

This is a portfolio / decision-support demonstration, not a substitute for a FEMA Preliminary Damage Assessment.

Disaster Obligation Estimator

An independent, transparent estimator of likely FEMA PA/IA obligated dollars for upper-Midwest (Region 5) events, built entirely on public open data.

Data sources

  • OpenFEMA — FemaWebDisasterSummaries (v1)
  • OpenFEMA — DisasterDeclarationsSummaries (v2)
  • Hazard proxies derived from declaration narratives
  • FY2026 FEMA declaration indicators

Use it responsibly

  • Not for eligibility / rights determinations
  • Estimates are illustrative — not advice
  • Verify against FEMA PDAs & official records
