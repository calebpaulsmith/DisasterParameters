How this works

Real data, measured hazards
  • Obligations (real): PA & IHP dollars from OpenFEMA, refreshed live.
  • Hazards (measured): peak wind, max hail, and tornado EF come from the NOAA Storm Events Database; peak river stage from USGS daily values — joined to each disaster by county + incident window. No more proxies.
  • Matching: your wind / hail / river-stage / tornado inputs are compared to each past event with a Gaussian kernel; rainfall & population scale the magnitude and declaration likelihood.
  • Honest intervals: disaster cost is skewed and long-tailed — point estimates carry wide ranges.
Limitations
  • Storm Events damage/hazard reports are NWS "best estimates"; USGS gage height is daily-mean (understates instantaneous crest); some events join at state level where county detail is missing.
  • Obligations lag and reconcile over years — recent disasters under-count.
  • Only declared events are modeled; declaration is partly bureaucratic; pending FEMA reform could change the regime.
